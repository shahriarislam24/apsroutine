Study Rules

Rule 1: No new topics if current isn't 70% understood

Better to go slower and build solid foundations than rush through and remember nothing. If you can't solve 7/10 practice problems confidently, don't move forward.

Rule 2: Every Sunday = Test yourself

Solve past paper questions on that week's topics. This is non-negotiable. It's your reality check.

Rule 3: Lazy days happen - don't double up

You budgeted 6 lazy days/month. When you miss a day, just continue the schedule next day. Trying to do 18 hours to "catch up" will burn you out.

Rule 4: Coaching conflicts = Practice time

If coaching covers a topic you're studying, use that session for solving problems instead of learning. Don't waste time re-learning what you know.

Rule 5: June 1 = Hard deadline for learning

All content must be learned by June 1st. June is ONLY for past papers. If you're still learning in June, you won't have enough practice time.

Study Session Structure

For every topic:

1. Learn (40%)

Read notes, watch videos, make summary notes

2. Practice (40%)

Solve 10-15 problems from textbook

3. Active Recall (20%)

Close books, write what you remember, self-quiz

Key Tips

  • Keep room cold during midnight study, take 10min breaks every 2 hours
  • Never compromise 8 hours sleep - brain consolidates learning then
  • Chemistry is weakest - give extra time on difficult topics
  • Keep mistake notebook - write every wrong answer with solution
  • Saturdays = review only, no new content
  • Use coaching for clarifying self-study struggles
